# Executive Summary

> Source: [Perforce State of DevOps 2026 — Executive Summary](https://www.perforce.com/resources/state-of-devops)

---

## Purpose and Scope

As organizations navigate the integration of AI into critical software delivery streams, executives face a pivotal question: **Does AI render traditional DevOps practices obsolete, or does it demand their evolution?**

Perforce Software's 2026 State of DevOps report analyzes survey data from over 800 IT professionals to determine the relationship between DevOps maturity, AI success, and economic performance. Respondents come from global organizations across high-intensity sectors such as financial services, healthcare, and technology.

**The central finding:** DevOps has not lost relevance in the age of AI — it has become the prerequisite for AI scalability. Incomplete DevOps practices remain the primary barrier to value realization.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. DevOps Maturity Drives AI Success

AI adoption cannot succeed in a vacuum. The data reveals a direct correlation between established DevOps maturity and the ability to leverage AI effectively.

- **70%** of organizations indicate that DevOps maturity meaningfully influences their AI success.
- **72%** of leaders among high-maturity organizations report deeply embedded AI practices, compared to just **18%** in low-maturity counterparts.
- High-maturity organizations are almost **twice as likely** as lower-maturity organizations to favor hybrid DevOps–platform engineering delivery models (**79% vs 45%**).

### 2. The Economic Advantage of Standardization

Operational consistency translates directly to economic productivity and cost control. Mature organizations minimize rework and accelerate recovery, while immature processes inflate operational expenses.

- **Automation ROI:** High-maturity organizations are **36% more likely** to automate the majority of deployments (at least 61%) from commit to production.
- **Incident Response:** These same organizations are **66% more likely** to respond "very effectively" to production incidents, utilizing automated rollbacks and clear processes to mitigate downtime.
- **The Cost of Inconsistency:** Low-maturity organizations face higher variability, with **78% operating non-standardized delivery models**. This leads to increased rework and cloud spend inefficiencies.

### 3. Governance and The "Confidence Gap"

A critical disconnect exists between perceived AI confidence and actual governance capabilities. While optimism is high, the mechanisms to verify and audit AI outputs remain underdeveloped.

- **Confidence vs. Verification:** While **77%** of respondents express confidence in AI outputs, only **38%** have AI deeply embedded across multiple delivery stages.
- **Compliance Risk:** Only **39%** of organizations maintain fully automated audit trails, limiting visibility into AI performance and compliance adherence.

### 4. Organizational Bottlenecks Outweigh Tooling Constraints

Contrary to common belief, the primary impediments to scaling are organizational, not tooling.

| Bottleneck | Share |
|---|---|
| Coordination and organizational silos | 25% |
| Skills gaps | 25% |
| Compliance requirements | 22% |
| Environment management | 18% |
| Tooling constraints | **10%** |

> Tooling is rarely the primary constraint. Cross-team coordination and governance are the real work of scale.

---

## Report Takeaways

### 1. AI Amplifies DevOps Maturity Gaps

While DevOps practices are widely adopted, their implementation varies significantly. AI accentuates these disparities, creating sharper divides in outcomes, reliability, and operational costs. This uneven maturity leads to a paradox where DevOps is simultaneously seen as both "solved" and "broken."

### 2. Compliance is an Essential Growth Enabler

Compliance should not be seen as a hindrance. Organizations should adopt integrated compliance solutions that streamline adherence while enabling faster delivery and reducing risks. Automating audit trails will help ensure that AI outputs meet rigorous compliance standards in regulated industries.

### 3. Focus on People, Process, and Advanced Environment Management

Addressing coordination friction and skills gaps remains key to achieving success with AI. Improved environment management tools and strategies reduce delays, enhance testing efficiency, and ensure smooth system cutovers, ultimately accelerating time-to-market.

---

## Bottom Line

> **AI amplifies existing organizational states. It accelerates success in mature DevOps environments and widens the risk profile in immature ones. Success with AI requires a commitment to maturing DevOps practices.**
>
> DevOps has not failed. **Incomplete DevOps has.**
