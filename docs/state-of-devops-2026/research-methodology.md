# Research Methodology & Respondent Profile

> Source: [Perforce State of DevOps 2026 — Research Methodology](https://www.perforce.com/resources/state-of-devops/research-methodology-respondent-profile)

---

## Survey Design

- **Total respondents:** 820 IT professionals
- **Panel source:** Leading global online panel provider
- **Selection method:** Geographic and role-based quotas + screening questions
- **Screening criteria:**
  - Active role in IT decision-making or purchase influence
  - Ability to accurately understand DevOps, platform engineering, and AI
  - Self-reported DevOps knowledge
  - Must be able to explain "Agentic AI" to a colleague
  - Attentiveness to survey questions

> Note: This is not a probability sample — a margin of error cannot be stated. Respondents were drawn from an online panel with unknown relationship to the total universe.

## DevOps Maturity Model Definitions

These tiers are the foundational taxonomy used across the entire report. All benchmark numbers are reported against these three groups.

### High-Maturity Organizations

Organizations that have **all three** of the following:
1. **Standardized delivery practices** — consistent, well-defined workflows across teams
2. **Highly effective incident response** — with automated rollback capabilities and clear processes
3. **≥ 61% deployment automation** — from commit to production

### Mid-Maturity Organizations

Organizations that have:
1. **Mostly-to-highly standardized practices** — generally consistent but lacking the full rigor of high-maturity
2. **Effective incident response** — functional but without full automation
3. **31–60% deployment automation** — meaningful automation but not yet dominant

### Low-Maturity Organizations

Organizations that have:
1. **Ad hoc or partially standardized practices** — team-dependent, inconsistent workflows
2. **Poor incident response** — limited tooling, reactive rather than systematic
3. **< 31% deployment automation** — manual-dominant delivery

## Distribution of Maturity Tiers (self-reported)

| Delivery Standardization Level | Share |
|---|---|
| Highly standardized + strong automation + governance | **32%** |
| Mostly standardized | **35%** |
| Partial or ad hoc ("depends on the team") | **34%** |

The 34% "depends on the team" segment is where AI scaling stalls in practice.
